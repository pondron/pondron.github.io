The Problem

Linear thinking has led to linear progress.

At its core, education is the encoding and storage of information in the brain. Advances in neurology have decoupled from advances in education, and this fundamental principle has been lost. Students learn in a linear fashion as the have done for hundreds of year, even as the Connectome was mapped revealing the true structure of the brain (hint hint it is not linear).

The Solution

Unlocking the brains potential.



Platform(Connectome):
A visual representation of core concepts and their relationships to each other. These core concepts are broken down and form the nodes of our graph(neurons). The relationships between these concepts form the edges(synapses), and the leaner has a final conceptual map that mimics the circuitry of their own brain.


Recursive Learning:
With the concept connectome as the learner's map(syllabus), we walk the relationship paths as we explore a concept. As the curriculum walks these paths the adjacent relationships are registered as associated to the current edge. The the learners visual cortex encodes the information this stimuli in a format that the brain already understands, Memory Traces
With the use of Spaced Repetition the learner walks the entire concept connectome, coloring the edges as they go, and encodes the entire graph in a fraction of the time with a more robust recall.
